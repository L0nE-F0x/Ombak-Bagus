What you get

Everything you check before you go

A personal Bali desk - not a social feed, not a paywalled portal. Conditions, spots, and the notes you actually trust.

Live swell & wind

Hourly height, period, direction, and wind relation - scored against each break's orientation and skill band.

Multi-model compare

Cross-check Best match, GFS Wave, ECMWF WAM, and Meteo-France - the multi-model habit, without the spreadsheet feel.

Primary + secondary swell

See combo seas when a second train is in the grid - useful on Bukit and east-coast days when the mix matters.

Tide charts

Model sea level when available, plus an astronomical estimate so you can plan mid-tide windows with a second opinion.

16 curated Bali spots

Uluwatu, Padang, Bingin, Canggu peaks, Keramas, Medewi, and more - each with swell windows and offshore angles.

Notes & session log

Local knowledge and post-session notes stay on your machine. Favorites, units, and history persist between launches.

System tray

Minimize or close to tray. Left-click to reopen, right-click to quit - one click from the lineup check.

Free data stack

Powered by Open-Meteo marine & weather APIs - no account, no API key for personal use. Model names stay transparent in Settings.

FAQ

Quick answers

Is it really free?

Yes for personal use. Forecast data comes from Open-Meteo's free non-commercial APIs. The app itself is a personal project.

Does it only work for Bali?

The spot catalog and scoring are curated for Bali breaks. That's the point - a desk tool for living here, not a global directory.

Is this as good as Windguru?

Windguru is excellent for multi-model tables. Ombak Bagus brings multi-model swell comparison plus Bali-specific spots, scores, tides, notes, and sessions into one native window. Use both if you like.

Where does my data go?

Favorites, notes, and sessions stay on your PC. Forecasts are fetched live from Open-Meteo and are not uploaded to us.
